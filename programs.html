Impact & Programs.

Everything we do at Cucharadas de Amor is designed to improve the quality of life for the families we serve, building academic, personal, and social skills that lead to independence and lasting change.

Our Programs

Education

Our education program strengthens children's academic skills, giving them the tools to continue their schooling and build better futures.

  • Academic tutoring for elementary-school children to help them catch up and stay on track.
  • Full elementary education for children ages 7–13 who do not have a place in a school.
  • Enrollment in the INEA program for children ages 10–14 who need to regularize their education.

Personal Development

This program strengthens personal and family growth through activities that promote values, social skills, and emotional well-being.

  • Workshops and courses led by professionals, covering self-esteem, respect, dignity, tolerance, and responsibility.
  • A parenting school designed to strengthen parenting skills and promote healthy family environments.

Nutritional Support

Our nutritional support program helps ensure food security for children and their families, providing meals that support well-being and academic performance.

  • Breakfast or lunch provided to children who attend educational activities.
  • Subsidized food baskets distributed to families participating in workshops and courses.

Testimonials

"I believe every child who comes to Cucharadas de Amor carries something meaningful in their heart. Odette grew up surrounded by violence, addiction, and loss — a reality that often repeats itself across generations. But she chose a different path. Today, she has a job, dreams for her future, and a life built on strength and resilience."
— Gabriela Maldonado, Director
"One moment that stayed with me was when a girl said, 'At Cucharadas de Amor, they listen to me.' Many of these children grow up with their voices unheard and their needs overlooked. That's why those simple words meant everything. Here, they are seen as individuals — as children who deserve to be heard, valued, and cared for."
— Anna, Nutritionist
"When I first met him, he barely spoke — just quiet nods and short answers, as if he was afraid to be seen or heard. A year later, he ran up to me with a huge smile and called my name. In that moment, I realized he finally felt safe, seen, and confident enough to express himself."
— Karla Paola, Admin

Working Alongside Our Community

We work alongside other organizations and institutions to maximize our positive impact. Every program is designed to reduce educational gaps, build children's confidence, foster positive family relationships, and support the emotional development of all participants.
